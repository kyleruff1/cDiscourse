## 0. How this design was built

Same method as QOL-030 — **derived from node stages in timeline mode and the
storyboard narratives.** §3 fixes the entry catalogue against the timeline; §4
fixes ordering against node stages; §5 replays the narratives to prove the
catalogue is complete. The Act popout is the user-facing face of QOL-030's
`buildActPopout` pure function.

## 1. Goal & scope

The **Act popout** is the universal contextual action menu — "what can I do
here." It converges three shipped surfaces into one popout:

- **SC-001** side action rail
- **SC-004** timeline node action dock
- **RULE-005** channel chip row

In scope: the full entry catalogue, the three entry kinds, anchoring, stage-
driven ordering, the disabled-with-reason rule, the convergence mapping. Out of
scope: the chassis itself and `buildActPopout` (QOL-030); keyboard keys
(reopened IX-003 — keys are *shown* here, defined there).

## 2. Inputs

QOL-030's `Popout` / `PopoutEntry` / `PopoutGroup` chassis and the
`buildActPopout(target, role, stage, engine)` pure function. The Act popout is
that function's output rendered through the chassis. RULE-005 `channelModel`,
the role matrices, and `engine.ts` are consumed read-only.

## 3. The Act popout in timeline mode

### 3.1 Anchoring

The Act popout anchors to its **target**:

| Target | Anchor |
|---|---|
| a node | the selected node on the rail |
| an evidence object | the evidence pill on its node |
| a concession set | the concession sub-items on its node |
| a branch | the branch stub |
| the room | the room title strip (room-level entries) |
| none (new room) | the minimized box dock |

It opens fast (120–160 ms; reduce-motion instant), never hides the board behind
it, and dismisses on `Esc`, scrim tap, or entry selection.

### 3.2 Three entry kinds

| Kind | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| **box-opening** | sets the box `(type, target)` and opens the OneBox |
| **direct** | acts immediately — no box opens |
| **role-change** | changes the viewer's seat, then re-opens the Act popout |

### 3.3 Stage-driven ordering

The Act popout's groups always render in the fixed order **Respond · Evidence ·
Resolve · Structure · Direct · Role**, but the **promoted entry** — pulled to
the top of its group and visually emphasized — is chosen by the target node's
LIFE-001 stage, per QOL-030 §3.4. The stage **only orders**; it never removes a
valid entry. Engine-invalid entries are *removed*; role-blocked entries are
*shown disabled with a one-line reason* — never a silent omission.

## 5. Convergence — what folds in

| Shipped surface | Folds into | Reused unchanged |
|---|---|---|
| **SC-001** `ArgumentSideActionRail` | the Act popout's Direct + Role groups | the role matrices (`ObserverActionDockLayout`) — the §4 role gating |
| **SC-004** `TimelineNodeActionDock` | the Act popout's box-opening groups | `timelineNodeActionDockModel` — the action→preset mapping |
| **RULE-005** `ChannelChipRow` | the Act popout's box-opening entries | `channelModel` — the channel→type mapping |

All three are thin React shells over pure models; QOL-031 **re-houses the
shells** under one popout and reuses the models. The collapsed `ArgumentSide
ActionRail` becomes the Act popout's *trigger* (the minimized affordance that
opens it).

## 8. Edge cases

- **Engine yields zero box-opening entries** (locked / archived node) — only
  Direct entries (Share, Watch) render; the box never opens.
- **Own node** — box-opening + Flag removed; only qualifiers + Request deletion.
- **Primary seat open** — the Govern group is hidden (no one to govern with),
  per GAME-005.
- **Stale popout** — a stage change while the popout is open re-derives it on
  the next render; selection of a now-invalid entry is a no-op with a toast.

## 9. Test plan

- The §4 catalogue — every entry's kind, group, target-kinds.
- The 3-gate output (delegated to QOL-030's `buildActPopout` test) — QOL-031
  asserts **rendering**: group order fixed, promoted entry emphasized, disabled
  entries visible with a reason.
- Narrative-frame tests — the §6 table reproduced.
- Convergence — the SC-001/SC-004/RULE-005 models still drive the same actions.
- Doctrine ban-list scan; chassis a11y (focus trap, 44px, `Esc`).

## 10. Doctrine & safety self-check

- The stage gate only orders — never removes a valid entry (QOL-030 §3.3).
- Disabled entries always show *why* — no silent omission.
- Deterministic pure content model — no AI, no network, no heat input.
- Direct entries that mutate (Flag, Make-private, governance) go through
  existing Edge Functions / RLS — no service-role in client.
- No verdict / winner / loser copy in any entry label.

## Implementer note (QOL-031 build) — resolved against the shipped model

The §9 test plan is explicit: QOL-031 is the **rendering** of QOL-030's
shipped `buildActPopout`, not the model. Two points were resolved against
the *shipped* `actPopoutModel.ts` rather than the §4 aspirational catalogue:

1. **§4 catalogue vs. the shipped entry set.** The shipped `buildActPopout`
   does not enumerate `Mark moved-on` / `Mark ignored` / `Share` /
   `Acknowledge / Touché` or the four-entry `Govern` group from §4. Per the
   card's "consumed, not re-derived" instruction the render layer renders
   exactly the model's `ActEntryId` set; the shipped model folds governance
   into the `Direct` group. Surfacing the missing §4 entries is a change to
   `actPopoutModel.ts` (a QOL-030-owned chassis file) and is therefore a
   **separate follow-up** against QOL-030, not a QOL-031 render-layer task.

2. **§3.3 "role-blocked shown disabled with a reason."** The shipped engine
   + role gates *remove* invalid / forbidden entries — they do not emit a
   disabled entry. QOL-031's `ActPopout` renders the disabled-with-reason
   case via an additive host-supplied `disabledEntries` map (an entry the
   model produced but the live context makes unavailable — the §7
   wireframe's "Flag — disabled: can't flag your own"). This satisfies §3.3
   / §7 / §8 without modifying the model or the chassis. A model that emits
   disabled entries directly would be a QOL-030 follow-up.

Neither point is a defect — both are render-layer resolutions inside the
card's stated scope. No redesign was performed.
